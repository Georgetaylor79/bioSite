Life of Lena Taylor

Place of Birth

grew up in the era of Jim Crow and discrimination in the south. That motivated her to learn very quickly, study hard and follow in her mother’s footsteps, becoming a teacher. Her life on the farm taught her values and skills that she still uses to this day. Having siblings helped her build people skills that allow her to work well with others personally and professionally. Graduating college from < href="https://www.jsums.edu/">Jackson State University and later moving up north to pursue her career in teaching others. There she would find the love of her life and start a family of her own.

Family Journey

She was 1 of 10 children born in a small town outside Jackson, Mississippi. Something she shared with her late husband. They were both from Mississippi and understood the effort it took to become successful early. They were married over 30 years and had two children in the journey of life together. She also became a grandmother as well. She would work for the school district of Wisconsin for over 25 years. She enjoys donating back to her alumni.

Additional Background Information

While growing up she was given a nickname that she was not fond of and because of that experience she as always called people by their prefer name. She doesn’t believe in making fun of others. She thinks you should always give it your best. She gives motivational speeches on how we should always try our best and help others.

She plans on enjoying life to its fullest and learning new things along the way.
